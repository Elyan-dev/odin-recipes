Beans Recipe

Description

Making a pot of beans from scratch is a simple way to get a versatile, high-protein base for your meals. A traditional Mexican-style Frijoles de Olla (beans from the pot) results in tender beans with a rich, flavorful broth that can be eaten on its own or mashed later for refried beans.

Ingredients

  • Dry pinto beans
  • Water or Broth
  • 1/2 Onion
  • 3-4 whole garlic cloves
  • Fat(Optional) 1-2 tbsp lard, oil
  • 1-2 tbsp salt and a bay leaf

Basic preperation steps

  1. Sort and Rinse: Spread the dry beans out and remove any small stones or shriveled beans. Rinse thoroughly in a colander under cold water.
  2. Soak (Optional but Recommended): Place beans in a large bowl with enough water to cover them by 2-3 inches. Soak for 8 hours or overnight to reduce cooking time and improve digestibility. Drain and rinse before cooking
  3. Simmer: Place the beans, water/broth, aromatics, and fat in a large heavy pot. Bring to a rolling boil, then reduce heat to low for a gentle simmer.
  4. Cook until Tender: Cover with a lid (slightly ajar) and simmer for 1.5 to 3 hours. Check occasionally to ensure the beans stay submerged; add more hot water if the level gets too low.
  5. Salt at the End: Once the beans are soft to the touch, stir in the salt. Simmer for another 15 - 30 minutes to allow the salt to penetrate.
  6. Serve or Store: Remove the whole onion, garlic, and bay leaf. Enjoy immediately or store in the fridge with their cooking liquid for up to 5 days
