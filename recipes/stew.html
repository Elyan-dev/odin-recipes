Stew Recipe

Description

A classic beef stew is a hearty, one-pot meal where tough cuts of meat become fork-tender through a slow simmering process in a savory broth with root vegetables. It is a highly nutritious option for your fitness routine, providing approximately 25–30g of protein per bowl to aid in muscle recovery.

Ingredients

  • beef chuck roast
  • Vegetables (carrots, celery, onion)
  • 4 cups of beef broth, 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup flour, 2 tbsp oil, 3 cloves minced garlic, 1 bay leaf, 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 cup frozen peas and 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce

Basic preperation steps

  1. Sear the Beef: Pat the beef dry and toss with flour, salt, and pepper. Heat oil in a Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Brown the beef in batches until a dark crust forms, then set aside.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: In the same pot, add the onion, carrots, and celery. Cook until slightly softened (5 minutes). Stir in the garlic and tomato paste for another minute.
  3. Deglaze the Pot: Pour in the wine or a splash of broth. Use a wooden spoon to scrape up the flavorful brown bits (fond) stuck to the bottom.
  4. Slow Simmer: Return the beef to the pot. Add the broth, bay leaf, thyme, and Worcestershire sauce. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 1.5 hours until the meat begins to soften.
  5. Add Potatoes: Stir in the cubed potatoes. Cover and continue simmering for another 60 minutes until both the meat and vegetables are completely tender.
  6. Thicken and Finish: If the stew is too thin, stir in a cornstarch slurry (1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 1 tbsp water). Add the frozen peas during the last 5 minutes of cooking.
